Fonce River

The Fonse River, located in the town of San Gil, Santander, is one of Colombia’s top destinations for rafting. Its crystal-clear waters, narrow channel, and series of level II and III rapids provide an exciting yet safe experience, ideal for both beginners and those with some experience. The route takes about one to two hours, depending on the water level, and winds through lush vegetation and scenic mountain landscapes. Local tour operators provide all necessary equipment, certified guides, and transportation, making this activity accessible, well-organized, and perfect for anyone seeking adventure in nature.

Suárez River

The Suárez River, also located near San Gil in the Santander region, is known for offering one of the most intense rafting experiences in Colombia. With rapids ranging from level III to V, it is ideal for people with previous rafting experience or those looking for a high-adrenaline adventure. The river features powerful currents, large waves, and thrilling drops that make the descent both challenging and exciting. The journey typically lasts around two hours and covers a stretch of river surrounded by impressive natural scenery. Due to its difficulty, safety is a priority—agencies provide professional guides, top-quality equipment, and a detailed briefing before starting.

Chicamocha Canyon

The Chicamocha Canyon, located in the heart of Santander, Colombia, is one of the most breathtaking natural wonders in the country and a popular destination for adventure tourism. Carved over millions of years by the Chicamocha River, the canyon stretches over 200 kilometers and reaches depths of more than 2,000 meters, making it one of the largest canyons in the world. Its dramatic cliffs, dry tropical forest, and panoramic views offer a stunning backdrop for outdoor activities such as paragliding, hiking, and cable car rides. Visitors can enjoy the landscape from the Chicamocha National Park (Panachi), which provides tourist services, ecological trails, and cultural exhibits.

San Francisco to Río Verde

The route from San Francisco to Río Verde, located in the department of Cundinamarca, Colombia, is a hidden gem for nature lovers and hiking enthusiasts. This trail takes you through lush cloud forests, waterfalls, and scenic viewpoints, offering a peaceful escape from the city and a chance to connect with nature. The hike is moderately challenging, with steep sections and muddy paths, especially during the rainy season, but the reward is worth it. Along the way, hikers can enjoy the sound of birds, the freshness of the forest, and the beauty of small streams that eventually lead to the Río Verde, a pristine river known for its clear waters and tranquil atmosphere.
